# FAM Fundamentals of Actuarial Mathematics

## Exam Format
- **Number of Questions**: 34 multiple-choice questions
- **Time Limit**: 3.5 hours (210 minutes)
- **Format**: Computer-Based Testing (CBT) via Prometric
- **Calculator**: TI-30XS MultiView or BA II Plus approved models
- **Passing Score**: Scaled score of 6 out of 10
- **Frequency**: Offered 3 times per year (March, July, November)

## Exam Structure

This is a two-part exam covering both short-term and long-term actuarial mathematics:

### Short-Term Section
- Introduction to modeling methods
- Foundational principles of ratemaking
- Reserving for short-term coverages
- Frequency and severity models
- Aggregate loss models

### Long-Term Section
- Survival models and life tables
- Life insurance models
- Life annuities
- Premium calculation
- Reserves and policy values

## Exam Topics

### Short-Term Topics
- Frequency distributions (Poisson, negative binomial, binomial)
- Severity distributions (exponential, gamma, Pareto, Weibull, lognormal)
- Coverage modifications (deductibles, policy limits, coinsurance)
- Aggregate loss models
- Risk measures and credibility

### Long-Term Topics
- Survival distributions and life tables
- Life insurance (whole life, term, endowment)
- Life annuities (immediate, due, deferred)
- Net premiums and premium reserves
- Multiple life functions
- Multiple decrement models

- Few pilot questions randomly placed (not scored)
- All unanswered questions scored as incorrect - answer every question
- PDF exhibits available during exam via "Exhibit" button
- Normal distribution calculator provided in CBT environment

## Study Tips

- Average study time: 300-400 hours
- This is typically the third or fourth exam in the ASA sequence
- Both short-term and long-term sections require equal attention
- Excel proficiency helpful for certain questions
- Build strong foundation in survival models and loss distributions
- Practice working problems under time pressure (approximately 6 minutes per question)
